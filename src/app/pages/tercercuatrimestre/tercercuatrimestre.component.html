Sillicon Valley is a region in the southern San Francisco Bay Area of Northern California which serves as a global center for high technology, innovation, and social media. It corresponds roughly to the geographical Santa Clara Valley. San Jose is the Valley's largest city, the third largest in California, and the tenth largest in the United States. Other major Silicon Valley cities include Palo Alto, Menlo Park,Cupertino, Santa Clara, Mountain View, and Sunnyvale.[1] The San Jose Metropolitan Area has the third highest GDP per capita in the world (after Zurich, Switzerland and Oslo, Norway), according to the Brookings Institution. Silicon Valley's first internationally known art gallery, Pace Art and Technology Gallery in Menlo Park, opened on February 6, 2016. In 1928, the Allied Arts Guild was formed in Menlo Park and is a complex of artist studios, shops, restaurant, and gardens.

The Starry Night

The Starry Night is an oil on canvas and possibly the most famous work of art by the Dutch post-impressionist painter Vincent van Gogh. Painted in June 1889, it depicts the view from the east-facing window of his asylum room at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, just before sunrise, with the addition of an idealized village. It has been in the permanent collection of the Museum of Modern Art in New York City since 1941, acquired through the Lillie P. Bliss Bequest. Regarded as among Van Gogh's finest works, The Starry Night is one of the most recognized paintings in the history of Western culture.

How To Make a Videogame

Follow this steps to create your own game

Author: David Montores Escalante
Published: November 2018

Step 1

Download free, easy to use game design software.  No matter what kind of game you're designing, you’ll need software to create it. Look for software with simple programming features so you don’t have to write individual lines of code. Look at the features of the software and download the one that works best for you. Some software that you can easily build your first game in include GameMaker, Twine, RPG Maker, Scratch, and Stencyl. The programs offer free downloads, but they may put watermarks in your game. If you want to make a 3D game, try using Unity. You will need to make 3D models for characters and levels using other software, such as Blender or Maya.

Step 2

Follow along with beginner video tutorials for your software.  Look up videos and walkthroughs on how to work with the game design software you downloaded. Many of the tutorials will walk you through the basic functions of how to program your characters and levels to do simple functions. Familiarize yourself with the program as best as you can. Check the software’s website to see if they have any official manuals or tutorials. That way, you’ll be learning from the people who know the software best. If you don’t like how one program works, try another until you find something that you like.

Step 3

Practice by making a simple game. Once you learn a program from tutorials, try to recreate a simple game, like Pong, Pac-Man, or Snake. Only use the basic art that the software provides and use what you learned from the video tutorials you watched to help program the game. Once you’re finished, test the game to see if it works. Only try to make simple games with easy gameplay. That way, you can test out the basics of the program for yourself. If you’re stuck, look up videos or walkthroughs on how to recreate the game. Set a deadline for yourself, such as completing the project in 1 week, so you don't spend too much time on it.

Step 4

Pick a genre for your game. Each type of game has rules or design elements that need to be included. Choose a genre that you enjoy playing and want to create yourself. Once you pick one for your game, focus on playing games in that genre to get a better understanding of what mechanics you should include. Common genres for games include first-person shooters, platformers, role-playing games, and puzzle games. Try putting 2 genres together to make an interesting combination of games. For example, you may make a puzzle game that’s also a platformer. Play a variety of games to see what you might want to include in your own.

Step 5

Play your game to make sure it functions how you want it. Once you finish building your prototype, export the file to a playable game. Test out your levels to make sure your characters and levels are behaving as you want them to. Take notes on your game of things that you need to fix, such as character sprites falling through the level’s floor. Try pressing a few buttons at the same time to see how the actions interact with one another.

Step 6

Add sound effects and music to the game. Sounds and music put the finishing touches on your game and make it feel complete. Look for royalty-free sound effects and music online if you don’t want to create your own. Otherwise, record and edit the sounds. Put a sound action in your programming so the sounds play at the right time. Websites like http://www.freesound.org offer community-recorded sounds that you can use in any project.
